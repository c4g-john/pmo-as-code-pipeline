## Problem Statement

Onboarding a new customer takes a median of 14 days and generates a high volume
of manual setup tickets, delaying time-to-value and inflating support cost.

## Options Considered

1. Do nothing — costs continue to rise with customer growth.
2. Add headcount to the manual setup team — scales cost linearly.
3. Build a self-serve onboarding flow — higher upfront cost, structural payback.

## Recommendation

Option 3: build the self-serve onboarding flow. It is the only option that
breaks the linear tie between customer growth and setup cost.

## Costs

Approximately $1.2M over the year, covering build, data migration, and support
during rollout.

## Benefits

Cutting onboarding to under 2 days is expected to reduce setup-related support
tickets by 80% and improve early-life retention. These benefits map directly to
the Aurora charter's success criteria.
